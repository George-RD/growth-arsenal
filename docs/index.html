01

A yes-man can't grow your business.

Ask most AI to check your offer and it nods. It agrees with you, then wishes you luck.

Agreement feels good. It teaches you nothing. Your real customers won't be so kind. They will just walk.

growth-arsenal does the walking away first. A sceptical marketer, a margin-obsessed strategist and a room of stand-in customers pick your idea apart while a fix still costs you nothing.

By the time you go to market, the worst objections are already dead.

03

How the stress test works

STEP 01

Draft

You bring the rough idea. The workshop asks the hard questions and builds a first version with you.

STEP 02

Attack

Adversarial agents hit it. A sceptic doubts the value. A strategist checks the margins. Stand-in customers say no.

STEP 03

Fix

You rework the weak spots while they're cheap. Every phase saves to a file, so a restart never loses your place.

STEP 04

Ship

What comes out has already survived its harshest audience. Now it meets the real one.

Not a chatbot with extra steps. A plain chat call skips the fight. Here, every phase gets torn apart before you move on.

05

Straight answers

Isn't this a chatbot with extra steps?

No. A plain chat call skips the fight. Every phase here gets torn apart by a sceptic, a strategist and stand-in customers before you move on.

Does this replace talking to real customers?

No. It kills the obvious weak spots first, cheaply, before real buyers see them. You still have to talk to real people. They just meet a stronger offer.

Do I need to read the Hormozi books first?

No. The workshop asks the questions and builds the offer with you, one phase at a time.

Can I run just the lead-gen part?

Yes. Each skill stands alone. The leads workshop will nudge you to do the offer first, but it won't block you either way.

Will the copy sound like AI wrote it?

No. Every customer-facing line runs through business-copy-style. Plain words. Short sentences. No robot tells.

Your offer meets real customers either way. Meet them ready.
